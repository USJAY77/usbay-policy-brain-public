# Outreach Sequence

Connection Request Message:

```text
Hi [Name], I’m working on AI governance infrastructure focused on human oversight, fail-closed enforcement, provenance, and audit-ready evidence. I’d be glad to connect with others thinking seriously about enterprise AI controls.
```

First Outreach Message:

```text
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

I’m currently speaking with enterprise teams about how they govern AI workflows once they move beyond experimentation.

The main areas I’m focused on are human approval, policy enforcement, provenance, and audit evidence.

Curious whether AI governance or controlled AI deployment is an active topic for your team this year.
```

Follow-Up 1:

```text
One pattern I’m seeing is that many teams have AI policies, but fewer have enforcement paths that can prove whether an AI action was approved, blocked, or policy-compliant at runtime.

That creates risk when AI moves into enterprise-critical workflows.

Are you seeing more pressure from security, compliance, or risk teams around AI evidence and oversight?
```

Follow-Up 2:

```text
I’m looking for a small number of enterprise teams to speak with about pilot use cases for governed AI execution.

The focus would be practical:

- Where AI actions need approval
- What evidence must be captured
- What should fail closed
- What audit trail is required
- How provenance should be preserved

Would a short conversation be useful?
```

Follow-Up 3:

```text
No pressure if this is not a current priority.

I’ll continue sharing practical notes on AI governance, human oversight, and audit evidence. If controlled AI deployment becomes more relevant for your team, I’d be glad to compare notes.
```

Inbound Reply:

```text
Thanks, [Name]. The best starting point is usually one workflow where AI is close to a meaningful business decision.

The questions I’d want to understand are:

- What action is AI assisting or triggering?
- Who needs to approve it?
- What policy applies?
- What evidence must be retained?
- What should happen if approval or provenance is missing?

Happy to walk through that with you.
```
